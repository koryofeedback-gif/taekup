Based on the **Sensei Academy** interface and the "Legacy" gamification model we established, here is the best way to split the content between Free and Premium.

The goal is to make the **Free** version useful enough to build a habit, but make the **Premium** version necessary for *promotion* and *faster leveling up*.

### The "Curriculum & XP" Split Strategy

#### 1. Free Tier (The "Initiate" Access)

* **Content:**
* **The Basics:** "How to tie your belt," "Dojo Etiquette," and "Warm-up routines."
* **Current Belt Preview:** Only the **first 2 videos** of their *current* belt requirement (e.g., "Basic Stance" and "Punch 1").
* **PDFs:** All PDF guides (static content is less valuable than video).


* **XP Reward:**
* Standard XP (e.g., +10 XP per video).
* **Cap:** They can only earn XP from these videos once.

* **Content:**
* **Full Curriculum:** The complete video library for their belt (Forms/Poomsae, Sparring drills, Self-defense).
* **"Next Level" Peek:** Access to view the videos for the *next* belt level (kids love seeing what comes next).
* **Exclusive Skills:** "Cool" content like "Flying Kicks," "Board Breaking Tips," or "Weapon Training."


* **XP Reward:**
* **Double XP:** +20 XP per video.
* **Repeatable Practice:** Earn +5 XP every time they re-watch and practice (capped daily) to encourage grinding.
